Accessibility of Delivery services in USA

For Nationwide View

Package Delivery

Various package delivery providers, including UPS, USPS, FedEx, and Amazon, operate extensively across the United States. While companies such as UPS maintain a nationwide presence, Amazon's delivery services are predominantly concentrated in metropolitan areas and along major transportation corridors.

Grocery Delivery

Grocery delivery services are offered by multiple brands within a typical radius of 15 miles. Walmart leads with 5,234 locations providing extensive coverage, followed by Target (1,780 locations), Trader Joe’s (521 locations), and Whole Foods (517 locations).

Meal Delivery

Meal delivery services continue to expand, relying primarily on bicycles or cars for deliveries. These services are predominantly urban, contingent upon the proximity of delivery personnel and participating restaurants. While DoorDash focuses on smaller, localized areas, UberEats covers a broader geographic scope.

Delivery Service Gaps and Population Density

Regions in the United States that lack delivery services are often situated in geographically challenging areas, such as deserts or mountainous terrain. Additionally, even within certain proximities to urban centers, “delivery-deserts” can be identified—areas devoid of accessible delivery services.

Delivery Desert

An estimated 4 million U.S. households reside in food deserts, where access to grocery stores is limited. Delivery services could serve as an alternative in these regions. However, numerous areas still lack grocery and meal delivery options, leaving some food deserts without sufficient access to fresh and healthy food supplies.
